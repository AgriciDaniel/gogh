# Constraint Beats Coaxing

Confidence tag: EVIDENCE-BASED (synthesis of the design-theory sources; the mechanism is directly stated by the skill's own design).

## Compiled Truth

This is the load-bearing idea that ties the whole framework together.

> You fix mean-seeking models not by asking for beauty but by **narrowing the space they sample from**, and packaging that narrowing so it travels.

Every other concept is a corollary:

- [[AI Slop]] is convergence to the statistical mean. "Make it beautiful" prompts do not move the model off the median, because the median *is* what "beautiful" averages to in the corpus.
- So the intervention is **constraint**, not encouragement: forbidden-pattern lists ([[AI Tells (Forbidden Patterns)]]), committing to one direction ([[The Three Dials]], the [[Greenfield Build (Prompt 1)|Design Read]]), and hard thresholds ([[Hero Discipline]], [[The Three Locks]], the [[Em-Dash Ban]]).
- The constraints are grounded in real craft ([[Encoded Design Principles]]) so they raise quality rather than just adding noise.
- They are made enforceable by turning judgment into a self-run gate ([[Design Review as Infrastructure]] -> [[Pre-Flight Check (Section 14)]]).
- The narrowing is made *achievable* by an [[Image-First Pipeline]] that converts open-ended generation into bounded reproduction.
- And the whole package is made *portable* by the [[Agent Skills Format|Agent Skills and SKILL.md Format]] so it travels to any agent.

[[Leon Lin]] states the mechanism plainly in the skill's origin story: Anthropic's frontend guidance told the AI to "pick an extreme aesthetic" and "be creative," but *"LLMs are just based on probability. Without strict rules, they statistically default to the most likely patterns."* Taste Skill is the strict-rules answer to that observation.

**The honest caveat.** Constraint raises the floor; it does not guarantee the ceiling. Over-tight rules can also cargo-cult (see [[Scope and Context]]) or chase trends (a "frontend taste checklist can become trend-chasing" - Developers Digest). Constraint is a default, not a law - the same caveat that applies to the [[Encoded Design Principles]].
