# Hero Discipline

Confidence tag: EVIDENCE-BASED (canonical `SKILL.md` §4.7; `tasteskill.dev` Quick Reminders).

## Compiled Truth

The hero must fit the initial viewport and stay restrained. This is one of the four required audits ([[Required Audits]]) and a heavily weighted part of the [[Pre-Flight Check (Section 14)]].

**Hard rules (§4.7):**
- **Headline: max 2 lines** at desktop. "A 4-line hero headline is always a font-size error, never a copy-length error."
- **Subtext: max 20 words AND max 3-4 lines.**
- **CTAs visible without scrolling.**
- **Hero top padding cap: `pt-24` (~6rem) max** at desktop.
- **Hero stack: max 4 text elements** - (1) eyebrow OR brand strip OR neither, (2) headline, (3) subtext, (4) CTAs (1 primary + max 1 secondary).
- **Hero font scale:** default `text-4xl md:text-5xl lg:text-6xl`; `text-6xl md:text-7xl` only for 3-5 word headlines.

**Banned inside the hero:** tagline below CTAs, trust micro-strip, pricing teaser, feature bullets, avatar row. A **"Used by" / "Trusted by" logo wall belongs UNDER the hero, never inside it.**

**CTA rules (§4.5):** primary CTA fits one line, **3 words max** (ideally 1-2); a wrapped CTA is a pre-flight Fail. **No duplicate-intent CTAs** on one page (pick one of "Get in touch" / "Contact us" / "Let's talk").

## Why

Directly encodes visual hierarchy and restraint from [[Encoded Design Principles]] - the hero is the single most important above-the-fold moment, and slop bloats it. See the audit: [[Hero discipline audit]] is run "in writing" with headline lines, subtext words, and CTA visibility called out.
