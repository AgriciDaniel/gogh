# Em-Dash Ban

Confidence tag: EVIDENCE-BASED (canonical `SKILL.md` §9.G, verbatim).

## Compiled Truth

The single most-emphasized rule in the skill, and "the single most-violated Tell." It is deliberately **binary and absolute**:

> "Em-dash (`—`) is COMPLETELY banned... There is no 'limited use' allowance, no 'natural language frequency' allowance, no 'in body copy is fine' allowance. None."

**What is banned:**
- The em-dash `—` (U+2014) **anywhere visible** - headlines, eyebrows, labels, pills, buttons, captions, nav, body copy, and quote attribution.
- The en-dash `–` (U+2013) as a separator - date and number ranges must use the plain hyphen `-`.

**What is allowed:** only the regular hyphen `-` and the math minus sign.

> "If your output contains a single `—` or `–` anywhere visible to the user, the output fails the Pre-Flight Check... The phrasing here is binary: zero em-dashes."

## Why it is here

The em-dash is a prose "AI tell" - the writing-side sibling of the visual [[AI Slop]] convergence. It is the first item on the [[Em-dash audit]] and a load-bearing box in the [[Pre-Flight Check (Section 14)]]. (Note: this brain's own house style, and the owner's global style, also avoid em-dashes - convenient alignment.)
